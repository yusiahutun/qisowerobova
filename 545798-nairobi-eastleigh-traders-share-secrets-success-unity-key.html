History of Eastleigh

According to Hiraa Online, Eastleigh was established in 1921. It was formally known as Nairobi East Township at the time.

Nairobi's residential estates were distributed according to race by the colonial administration, and Eastleigh was earmarked for Asians and upper-class Africans employed as clerks, builders, or shoemakers.

Class replaced racial and ethnic division as the primary means of segregation after the colonial government's initial attempts to do so in the Eastleigh neighbourhood put an end to the practice.

A sizeable Asian community previously inhabited Eastleigh before Somalis took over.

How Mohammed Saddat started a clothing business

Mohammed Saddat has been doing business in Eastleigh in Nairobi for over a decade.

The Wajir native came to the capital in 2009 after completing high school and seeking greener pastures. He scored a C—in the Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE).

Saddat did not proceed to a tertiary institution for further studies. Instead, he opened clothing and an M-Pesa shop in Eastleigh after he got a capital of KSh 20,000 from his mother and aunt.

There were few shops in the area then compared to the current influx.

Saddat now runs a more extensive wholesale shop, expanding his business following booming sales.

Speaking to TUKO.co.ke, he attributed the success of Eastleigh traders to unity.

"The secrets of Eastleigh traders are unity and supporting each other. They always pool resources together. Most of us put money together to import goods, thus reducing the losses," Saddat said.

Business Bay Square Mall, Eastleigh

The business hub unveiled one of Africa's largest malls in 2023 to meet growing business demand.

The Business Bay Square Mall (BBS) includes 3,500 shop spaces, 12 commercial banks, and seven-star hotels.

Why Eastleigh traders protested

In the recent past, Eastleigh traders accused the Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA) of unfairly imposing taxes on cargo, which has made it difficult for them to access their imported goods.

They blamed the government for the rise in cargo taxes, which they said was harming their businesses and the economy of Nairobi, as they were the largest taxpayers.

According to the traders, KRA was charging a 25% levy on imported goods, but they increased this to over 400%.

KRA inks deal with Eastleigh traders

KRA Commissioner General Humphrey Wattanga hosted the Eastleigh Business District Association (EBDA) chairman Ahmed Abdullahi on Monday, October 16, 2023.

Abdullahi welcomed KRA's move to mobilise revenue collection, noting Eastleigh traders were willing to pay their fair share of taxes.
