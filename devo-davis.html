Devo Davis is an American college basketball player who is catching the eyes of the leading media with his impressive performance in March Madness. His full name is Davonte Davis and plays for the Arkansas Razorbacks men’s basketball team of the Southeastern Conference (SEC). Devo is the main reason behind the successful run of his squad in the ongoing NCAA Tournament 2023. 

Early Life

Devo Davis was born on 24th September 2001, which makes him 21 years old as of 2023. Regarding family background, his father’s name is Terrie Davis and his mother’s name is not mentioned. His grandfather’s name is Yvette Davis. Talking about Devo Davis’s education, he studies in his hometown of Jacksonville, Arkansas. He graduated from Jacksonville Lighthouse School with good grades. Later, he went to the University of Arkansas for his higher educational degree. 

Devo Davis Career

Devo began his basketball career in his high school days. He played as a freshman. He averaged 16 points and 7 rebounds per game, leading his team to a regional championship and the Class 2A state semifinal. In his sophomore season, he averaged 18.4 points, 8 assists, 7 rebounds, and 2 steals per game. Later, he transferred to Jacksonville High School in Jacksonville. In his junior season, he averaged 18.5 points, 6 assists, and 5.3 rebounds per game.

He earned the honor of being called “the 2019 USA TODAY All-Arkansas first team”. In his senior season, he averaged 21 points, 10 rebounds, 8 assists, and 3 steals per game.  He was named 2020 Arkansas Democrat-Gazette Player of the Year. He played for Woodz Elite in the Nike EYBL (Elite Youth Basketball League circuit). At that time, he was acknowledged as a four-star player. His prospect ranked 80th on the ESPN Top 100 and 100th on the 247Sports Composite. He committed to playing college basketball for Arkansas Razorbacks men’s basketball team. 

2020-21 Season

He played as a Freshman in 30 games and 17 games started. He averaged 8.5 points, 2.1 assists, 4.5 rebounds, 47% (FG%), and 1.2 steals in 23.8 minutes per game. During the season, he was named to the SEC First-Year Academic Honor Roll.

2021-2022 Season

He played as a Sophomore in 37 games and 14 games started. He averaged 8.3 points, 2.8 assists, 3.7 rebounds, and 42.9% (FG%), in 27.6 minutes per game. During the season, he was named to the SEC Winter Academic Honor Roll.
