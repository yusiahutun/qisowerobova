Home of Leicester City Football Club

King Power Stadium is the proud home of Leicester City Football Club, a team that has risen to prominence in recent years, achieving remarkable successes in the Premier League.

Capacity and Attendance

The stadium has a seating capacity of 32,261, making it one of the smaller stadiums in the Premier League. However, the passionate fan base ensures that the stadium is regularly filled to capacity for home matches.

Formerly Known as the Walkers Stadium

Prior to a sponsorship deal with King Power, the stadium was known as the Walkers Stadium, named after the Walkers Crisps company.

Opening Date

The King Power Stadium was officially opened on 23 July 2002, with a friendly match between Leicester City and Athletic Bilbao.

Unique Fosse Style Architecture

The stadium’s design pays homage to Leicester City’s history. It features elements inspired by the Fosse Park, the club’s original ground.

State-of-the-Art Facilities

The stadium boasts modern facilities with luxurious hospitality suites, top-notch media facilities, and excellent viewing angles from all seats.

Expansion Plans

In 2015, the club unveiled plans for stadium expansion. The development aims to increase the capacity to over 40,000, allowing even more fans to witness the team’s matches.

European Nights

The King Power Stadium has witnessed some memorable European football nights, including Leicester City’s thrilling run in the UEFA Champions League in the 2016-2017 season.

Electric Atmosphere

The stadium is known for its incredible atmosphere, created by the passionate Leicester City fans who fill the stands and create a lively and loud environment on match days.

Statue of Vichai Srivaddhanaprabha

In tribute to the late chairman of Leicester City, Vichai Srivaddhanaprabha, a statue was erected outside the stadium. It serves as a reminder of his contributions to the club’s success.

Testimony of Success

The club’s incredible Premier League victory in the 2015-2016 season is symbolized by a special display embedded in the floor of the stadium’s main reception area.

The Leicester City Fanstore

Located within the stadium, the Leicester City Fanstore is a treasure trove for fans, offering a wide range of merchandise and memorabilia.

Stadium Tours

Fans have the opportunity to go on guided tours of the King Power Stadium, giving them an inside look at the players’ tunnel, the pitch, and other exclusive areas.

Conclusion

FAQs

1. How many seats does King Power Stadium have?

King Power Stadium has a seating capacity of 32,312.

2. What is the unique feature of King Power Stadium’s exterior?

The unique feature of King Power Stadium’s exterior is the large glass façade that wraps around the stadium, giving it a modern and transparent look.

3. Can I take a tour of King Power Stadium?

Yes, guided tours of King Power Stadium are available, allowing visitors to explore the team’s dressing room, pitchside, and other areas behind the scenes.

Yes, the stadium offers a range of amenities, including hospitality suites, lounges, and food and drink outlets, providing a comfortable and enjoyable experience for spectators.

5. How can I get to King Power Stadium?

King Power Stadium is easily accessible by public transportation, with several bus routes serving the area. Additionally, the stadium has parking facilities for those traveling by car.
