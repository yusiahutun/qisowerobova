Harry Maguire sends darts champ Michael Smith Man Utd invite despite transfer uncertainty

 Posted on June 16, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  470 words  |  Larita Shotwell

New world darts champion and Manchester United fan Michael Smith has been offered the red carpet treatment by Harry Maguire.

But Bully Boy may want to take up the offer sooner rather than later as speculation over the future of the Red Devils skipper, who is out of favour with manager Erik ten Hag, continues to rage.

The popular Smith has been inundated with messages from the world of sport since his stunning victory over Michael van Gerwen in the World final, where he also hit a memorable nine-dart finish in the greatest leg in the sport’s history. They have included messages from footballers Maguire and Real Madrid’s Toni Kroos, who is a darts fanatic.

Smith has taken the Sid Waddell Trophy to his beloved rugby league club, St Helens, and has made numerous other special appearances to mark his Ally Pally triumph.

And next up could be a trip to Old Trafford, provided Maguire is still at the club, with Aston Villa and Newcastle among the clubs linked with his services during the current transfer window.

“One time I was in the bathroom and my phone beeped. It was a message off Toni Kroos. I was like ‘mate, this is not real’,” he said in Bahrain ahead of the first World Series of Darts event of the year, which begins this afternoon (Thursday).

“I’ve been getting messages of Harry Maguire [saying] do you want to come to the training ground, the stadium?’. It’s mad.

“People I’ve looked up to, who didn't know who I am, have messaged me. Toni Kroos even sent me his number. That’s one of the highlights.”

Any plans for a trip to Old Trafford or Carrington may have to wait though as Smith continues a busy start to the year. After returning from the Middle East, he will head to Denmark for the Nordic Masters.

The Masters in Milton Keynes follows at the end of January before the start of the Premier League in Belfast on February 2.
