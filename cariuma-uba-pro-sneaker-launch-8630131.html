The Celeb-Loved Shoe Brand Cariuma Just Launched a Sell Out-Worthy Style

 Posted on April 15, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  390 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

Well, ladies, Cariuma has done it again. The celebrity-worn footwear label styled by Brooke Shields and Alexandra Daddario has officially added a new silhouette to its lineup of ultra-comfortable shoes. So, please, make some noise for the Uba Pro. 

The ‘90s-inspired sneaker fuses skateboarding and basketball shoe elements into one, with a sporty silhouette made of suede, mesh, rubber, and sugarcane. The durable street-ready shoe includes a perforated toe box, lace-up closure, flat, grippy sole, and bold colorblock design available in three variations. Better yet, the Uba Pro comes in sizes 5 to 13 — including half sizes — offering wearers the perfect fit.    

Cariuma Uba Pro Sneaker

The Uba Pro doesn’t only look cool; it feels cool (err, comfy), too. Like all of the brand’s shoes, comfort is at the forefront of the design, providing a foot-hugging, cloud-like experience with each step thanks to layers of cushioning, a bouncy midsole, and padded ankle back. No matter what your activities consist of (biking, walking, or weight lifting), you can rest assured knowing that your feet will be supported every step of the way.

As soon as I saw the launch hit the site, I knew I needed to get my hands — or, rather feet — on a pair. If they’re anything like the Oca Low sneakers I wear to the ground, I’ll rely on the new style like clockwork. Best of all, they mimic supermodel-approved styles, only furthering their allure. 

I haven’t even added the trainers to my cart yet, and I’m already thinking about styling the kicks with straight-leg jeans and a red baby tee. A white button-up top and slacks will also look super trendy for the office, while leggings, an oversized sweatshirt, and baseball cap make for an ideal casual weekend ensemble.  

Perhaps what I love most, though, is the environmental impact these kicks have. With every pair purchased, Cariuma plants two trees in the Brazilian rainforest, as part of its pair-for-pair program. So, you get to look good while also doing good for the planet — and that seems like a win-win to me.   

The only problem? The Uba Pro is sure to sell out given its cool silhouette, coveted comfort, and positive impact. In order to avoid that disappointment, be sure to secure your pair sooner rather than later.
