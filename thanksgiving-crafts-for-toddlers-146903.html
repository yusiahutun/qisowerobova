Thanksgiving Crafts for Toddlers Beyond a Handprint Turkey

 Posted on March 30, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  460 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

Finding great Thanksgiving crafts for toddlers can be a challenge. As any parent knows, many Thanksgiving craft ideas and fall crafts for kids are too complicated or time-consuming for adults to complete, let alone youngsters, which is never a good combo during the hectic holiday season.

That’s probably why the classic handprint turkey craft is the first thing that comes to mind for most people when they think of Thanksgiving crafts for kids. Don’t get us wrong; we adore turkey handprints of all shapes and sizes. The fun turkey decorations are so commonplace in schools and homes across America at this point that they may as well be a rite of passage instead of just fun crafts for kids. But because arts and crafts for kids are meant to encourage creativity in addition to celebrating big events like Turkey Day, we rounded up some of the best toddler Thanksgiving crafts beyond a handprint turkey that will challenge their growing minds and keep them busy so that you can focus on cooking a delicious Thanksgiving feast (or take a few quiet minutes for yourself to relax and watch the parade).

The Unexpected Benefits of Thanksgiving Crafts for Toddlers and Preschoolers

Arts and crafts for kids are vitally important to their development. Encouraging toddlers to express themselves through art is crucial for enhancing their creative thinking and problem solving skills later on, according to the Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC). Considering toddlers are also developing fine motor skills — the coordination of muscles, bones, and nerves to produce small, exact movements — it’s paramount that they have opportunities to practice and improve those, as well. Helping them with Thanksgiving activities is a wonderful place to start.

When it comes to toddler crafts for Thanksgiving in particular, well, the whole holiday is literally about giving thanks for what you have and teaching younger generations the importance of gratitude. So Thanksgiving crafts for kids give you an opportunity to not only boost your child’s physical and emotional development, but also to encourage important character traits, like gratitude, patience, and compassion. In other words, toddler crafts are something to be thankful for, as well.

But we also know that your time is limited on an average day, much less on one of the biggest holidays of the year that comes with a massive to-do list. Fear not: These easy crafts for kids won’t take you all day. In a matter of a few precious moments, you and the kiddo can put together something special that will make both of you proud and wow your guests on such a special day. Don’t miss the opportunity!
