Duster, a New Dress Label, Perfected the Day Dress

 Posted on March 6, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  704 words  |  Martina Birk

In My Most Worn, Bazaar editors share the fashion items they cherish more than any others in their closets. Here, fashion commerce editor Halie LeSavage explains how a dress from the new label Duster became her unofficial summer uniform.

A friend once told me that “busy” is the worst possible answer to the question, “How are you?” She reasoned that everyone has a lot going on; there are better, more thoughtful answers to give when someone’s checking in. Yet over the past month, “busy” is the only word I could think of to describe myself. I had begun a summer-long gauntlet of attending back-to-back-to-back weddings, covering a marathon of high-intensity fashion events at work (thank you, Nordstrom Anniversary Sale), and moving to a new home. This last item on the calendar opened a black hole of related errands and logistics I haven’t yet escaped—and had the potential to wreak havoc on my wardrobe.

90 percent of my closet was stuffed into boxes for the past month out of necessity; the other 10 percent I packed into my Away carry-on for easy access during the move. I expected to spend the month in matching workout sets and onesies, the better to roll luggage and answer emails on my mattress. But I also packed a button-front midi dress from the new brand Duster, just in case I wanted to feel more dressed up. In retrospect, this was the piece I wore more than all the others during my month of zero rest and relaxation.

Duster opened its virtual doors in late June, led by co-founders Melissa Magsaysay, a reporter, author, and luxury brand consultant, and Andrea Racey, founder of the silk brand Helena Quinn. Their joint venture specializes in dresses for women who want to look polished throughout whatever the day throws at them, with an emphasis on women's economic empowerment threaded into the designs. The dresses are inspired by the relaxed dusters traditionally worn by Filipino women for housework (Magsaysay is Filipino-American, and grew up seeing the women in her family wear them), reimagined with flourishes like structured necklines, bright fabrics, and twisted waist details for women working in various contexts today. Two percent of proceeds from each dress sold benefits Moms First, an organization creating policy proposals and advocacy campaigns to support working mothers.

This tie-in is personal for the founders. According to their brand-release story, the designers wanted to make dresses that could handle the demands of their lives as working moms—and give them a break from leggings and sneakers. As a result, the eight dresses in Duster's initial lineup emphasize versatility and comfort.

In my month on the move, I found that the dress I tried (the "All Things" duster, with short sleeves and a pointed collar) did exactly that. Duster’s button-up dress became my everything when I needed it. I darted from picking up cardboard boxes to catching early-bird writing shifts all in the same easy dress, only changing out the footwear from New Balance sneakers to leather sandals for dinner at the end of the day. The dress was comfortable and breathable in the peak June sunshine; pockets that could hold all the miscellaneous items I needed to transport from apartment to apartment were another plus.

Courtesy of Halie LeSavage

Wearing my Duster dress with Celine sunglasses, Reformation sandals, and a Janessa Leoné bag.

Most importantly, this dress was relaxed without feeling overly bucolic or nightgown-ish. The crisp poplin fabric and pointed collar made me feel like myself—polished and put-together on the outside—while everything else that was “me” was bubble wrapped, boxed up, and otherwise inaccessible.

Post-move, I hope to have better adjectives to describe myself than “busy.” Whatever tasks I must cross off my checklist or events to attend, I know I can handle them all wearing my new favorite dress. I hardly even mind that the rest of my closet hasn’t been unpacked yet.

Halie LeSavage is the fashion commerce editor at Harper's BAZAAR. Her style reporting covers everything from reviewing the best designer products to profiling emerging brands and designers. Previously, she was the founding retail writer at Morning Brew and a fashion associate at Glamour.
