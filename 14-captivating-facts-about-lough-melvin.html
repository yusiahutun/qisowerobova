The Unique Sligo-Leitrim Border

Lough Melvin is situated on the border of County Leitrim in the Republic of Ireland and County Fermanagh in Northern Ireland, making it a natural beauty shared by two different regions.

A Nature Lover’s Paradise

Surrounded by breathtaking landscapes and abundant flora and fauna, Lough Melvin is a haven for nature enthusiasts. Its serene atmosphere and diverse wildlife make it a perfect spot for bird watching and fishing.

Home to Rare Species

This picturesque lake is famous for its unique ecosystem, which includes rare fish species like the Arctic char, sonaghan, and gillaroo trout. These species are found only in a few select Irish lakes.

The Disappearing Islands

Lough Melvin boasts several islands dotted across its sparkling waters. However, some of these islands temporarily disappear during periods of heavy rainfall, giving the lake an ever-changing landscape.

The Great Fermanagh Cattle Drive

Every year, in a tradition dating back centuries, local farmers drive their cattle across the lake from County Fermanagh to graze in County Leitrim. It is a unique spectacle that showcases the strong connection between the two regions.

A Rich History

Lough Melvin has witnessed the footsteps of history, with ancient sites and ruins surrounding its shores. Castlecaldwell, one such ruin, offers a glimpse into the region’s past and provides a captivating backdrop to the lake.

A Hidden Gem for Anglers

Anglers from around the world flock to Lough Melvin to experience its legendary trout fishing. The lake is known for producing trophy-sized fish, and its reputation as a premier angling destination is well-deserved.

Serenity Amidst the Mountains

Located at the foot of the majestic Dartry Mountains, Lough Melvin offers a tranquil escape from the hustle and bustle of everyday life. The stunning mountain backdrop adds to the charm and serenity of the lake.

Historical Battle Sites

Several historical battles were fought near Lough Melvin in times gone by. These include the Battle of the Curlieus and the Battle of Doire Leathan, adding to the legends that surround the lake.

Enigmatic Crannogs

Lough Melvin is home to a number of crannogs, which are artificial islands that were once inhabited in ancient times. These enigmatic structures offer a glimpse into early Irish history.

FAQs

Q: What type of fish can be found in Lough Melvin?

A: Lough Melvin is home to a unique mix of both freshwater and saltwater fish species. It is famous for its prized trout, including the rare sonaghan trout, and also boasts salmon, char, and perch.

Q: Can I go fishing in Lough Melvin?

A: Yes, fishing is a popular activity in Lough Melvin. Anglers from around the world visit the lake to test their skills and try their luck at catching the elusive sonaghan trout or salmon. However, fishing permits are required, so be sure to obtain one before casting your line.

Q: Are there any hiking trails around Lough Melvin?

A: Yes, there are several hiking trails that surround Lough Melvin, offering stunning views of the lake and the surrounding countryside. These trails cater to various levels of difficulty, making them accessible to both novice and experienced hikers.

Q: Is swimming allowed in Lough Melvin?

A: While swimming is not prohibited in Lough Melvin, it is important to note that the water can be quite cold and unpredictable. It is advised to exercise caution and only swim in designated areas with lifeguards present.
