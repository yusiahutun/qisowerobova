Addiction Issues

Diego Maradona’s cocaine addiction is well-known. From the 1980s through 2004, he was a drug addict, which had a negative influence on his health and profession.

His position did not appear to improve when he suffered a major myocardial infarction in 2004 as a result of a cocaine overdose, despite moving to Cuba and attempting to pursue a drug recovery program.

In 1991, he tested positive for cocaine and was expelled from Napoli. After playing two games in the 1994 World Cup, he tested positive for ephedrine.

Health Problems

Even before retiring from soccer, Maradona’s health and physical fitness began to deteriorate.

His lifestyle began to spin out of control while at Napoli, and he spent the most of the weeks partying.

He quickly became obese, reaching a weight of 280 pounds at one time. To deal with his obesity, he eventually underwent gastric bypass surgery in 2005.

Maradona, on the other hand, continued to misuse alcohol and was hospitalized in 2007 owing to hepatitis and the negative effects of heavy drinking.

In 2018, while watching Argentina play in the FIFA World Cup, he collapsed in the stands and required paramedic assistance.

On November 25, 2020, Maradona died of a heart arrest at his home in Buenos Aires. Just two weeks before his death, Maradona had surgery to remove a blood clot from his brain.

Diego Armando Maradona, who passed away at the age of 60, is regarded as a football god. His manner of life, however, with his addiction demons, had crushed him.

Diego Maradona | Career

Diego Maradona made his professional debut with Argentinos Juniors in 1976, just before his sixteenth birthday. During his five years with the club, he played 167 games and scored 115 goals.

Boca Juniors signed him for $4 million in 1981 after he made an immediate impact at the national level.

Similarly, after a disappointing World Cup campaign, he only stayed with the side he grew up supporting for a year before transferring to Barcelona in 1982.

Following the World Cup loss, he was transferred to FC Barcelona in Spain for a world record fee of $5 million.

He was hampered by illness and injuries throughout his career with the club, despite winning the Copa del Rey and the Spanish Super Cup.

Meanwhile, he led Argentina to a World Cup victory in 1986. Throughout the tournament, he led his team from the front, asserting his influence in every game.

He scored five goals and made five assists in the quarter-finals, semi-finals, and finals against England, Belgium, and West Germany.

He continued to captain Argentina in the 1990 FIFA World Cup, with the only difference being that he was less powerful than before.

Similarly, he was named head coach of Argentina’s national team in 2008, a position he held for eighteen months until the 2010 World Cup.
