Early life

Robert William Barker came to this world on December 12, 1923. His biological father passed away when Bob was still young, so he lived with his mother, Matilda, until he got to eighth grade. He grew up in South Dakota, on the Rosebud Indian Reservation.

His mother would later remarry, and the family moved to Springfield, Missouri. Details of his siblings, if any, have remained under wraps. His stepfather also remains a mystery.

Education

Barker attended an unknown local high school before proceeding to join Drury College in Springfield. In 1943, he joined the United States Naval Reserve to train as a fighter pilot during World War 2. Unfortunately, the war ended before the TV host performed any active role. Years later, Bob went back to college and graduated in 1947 with an Economics degree.

Career

Barker's first job was at KTTS-FM Radio in Florida. He relocated to California in 1950 to pursue a TV career. Barker started hosting his radio show, The Bob Barker Show, which ran for six years. In 1956, the 97-year-old started hosting the daytime TV version of the radio show Truth or Consequences. He held this position until 1974 when the program was cancelled.

In 1996, Barker appeared as himself in the comedy Happy Gilmore alongside Adam Sandler. In the same year, Barker won an Emmy Award for Lifetime Achievement, one of the 14 Emmy Awards under his cap.

The Price Is Right

In 1972, Bob had started hosting another game show called The Price is Right. The program began airing in 1950 on NBC, then ABC, and eventually settled on CBS. The show had about 60 games which required the participants to guess the price of various products, from household items to luxury cars.

The Price Is Right became the first hour-long game show in TV history. In addition, in 1990, it surpassed Truth or Consequences as the longest-running daytime game show in history.

Thanks to his prowess, Bob Barker from Price is Right appeared in other shows and productions, including Pillsbury Bake-Off, where he was the MC, and the Tournament of Roses Parade, which he hosted between 1969 and 1988.

Marriage

Bob met Dorothy Jo Gideon, in high school when he was 15 years old. The two first met at an Ella Fitzgerald concert and started dating shortly afterwards. The young couple eloped while Bob was serving in the U.S Navy and got married in 1945.

Unknown to many, Gideon inspired Bob's passion for animal rights. As a result, she avoided fur and became a vegetarian, supporting her cause to defend animals.

In her honour, Bob started The DJ&T Foundation, named after Dorothy Jo and his mother, to help people pay to have their pets spayed or neutered. He also donates millions of dollars to animal rights organizations annually.

Unfortunately, Dorothy was diagnosed with lung cancer in 1981 and passed away after six months, ending their 36 years of marriage. Bob has shared that he never recovered from losing the love of his life and that he does not feel inclined to remarry. In his words, ''I just felt there should only ever be one Mrs Bob Barker.''
