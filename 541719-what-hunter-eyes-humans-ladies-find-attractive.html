What are hunter eyes?

What does it mean to have hunter eyes? The term "hunter eyes" describes a specific eye shape characterised by almond shape, positive canthal tilt, hooded eyelids, or minimal or no visible upper eyelids, creating a super intense gaze. They are also described as having no or minimally visible sclera below the iris, especially when looking straight ahead.

Hunter eyes distinctive traits

Hunter's eyes are associated with the following traits:

  • Almond shape: Oval-shaped with sharp, well-defined corners.
  • Positive canthal tilt: The outer corner of the eye is higher than the inner corner, forming a 'V' shape.
  • Hooded eyelids: The crease of your eyelid is non-existent or partially hidden under your protruding brow ridge (minimal or no visible eyelid).
  • Deep-set eyes: Your eyes are deeply positioned into your skull.
  • Minimal or no visible sclera: When looking straight ahead, there is little to no visible sclera below the iris.

Prey eyes distinctive traits

Prey eyes are associated with the following traits:

  • Negative canthal tilt: The inner corner of the eye is higher than or at the same level as the inner corner.
  • Visible sclera: There is a scleral show under the eyelid, which gives a more downcast look.

Generally, people with prey eyes give a more vulnerable look, while predator's eyes have a squinted, piercing look that screams dominance.

Are Hunter eyes genetic?

Yes, genetic factors like compact square eye orbital bones and a forward-developed maxilla cause an individual to have deep-set, alluring hunter eyes. However, with the development in science, you can easily get hunter eyes through surgery or muscle practice.
