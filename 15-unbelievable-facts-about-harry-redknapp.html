The 2nd-longest serving manager in Premier League history

Harry Redknapp holds the impressive record of being the second-longest serving manager in the history of the Premier League. With a managerial career that spanned over four decades, Redknapp has managed several clubs, including West Ham United, Portsmouth, Tottenham Hotspur, and Queens Park Rangers. His longevity and success in the Premier League are unparalleled.

One of the most remarkable achievements of Harry Redknapp’s career came in 2008 when he led Portsmouth to win the prestigious FA Cup. The team’s victory over Cardiff City in the final marked Portsmouth’s first major trophy in over 50 years. Redknapp’s tactical prowess and ability to motivate his players played a crucial role in their triumphant campaign.
