How to write an application letter for not attending an exam to a principal

 Posted on April 23, 2024  |  7 minutes  |  1374 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

Several reasons might cause a student to miss an exam, which is why knowledge of how to write an application letter for not attending an exam is essential. Whether a student is seeking to write a letter, or the parent is writing on behalf of their child, coming up with the best letter is fundamental. So, how is it written, and what points should you consider when writing this type of letter?

The general policy in many institutions is that exams cannot be postponed for situations occurring during the semester that cause a student to lose study time or miss exams. Hence, it is important to know how to explain yourself to your principal through a letter for missing an exam. How you explain your reason for not attending an exam is very important.

Your principal might understand your situation if you provide a genuine and solid reason and sound apologetic. An excuse letter for not attending exams might build trust and improves communication between you and your principal.

How to write an application letter for not attending an exam

There is nothing wrong with missing exams at the end of your physical classes or online classes. Therefore, if you have good reasons for not attending an exam, write a letter to your principal before they ask you for it. You will feel less nervous when explaining yourself on a piece of paper than when talking to your principal face-to-face.

Some principals are flexible enough to accept a short text message or call during emergencies but writing a letter or email for not attending an exam is professionally recommended. Here is the format:

  • Write the name of your school, location, and date.
  • Salute your principal with Dear Sir/Madam and add his or her official name.
  • Write the heading of your letter.
  • Go straight to the point regarding why you did not attend the exam.
  • Mention if you have attached pieces of evidence to the letter.
  • Apologize for any inconvenience you might have caused.
  • Show gratitude at the end by writing Thanks.
  • Write yours faithfully and official name. Make sure to sign your letter because your principal might keep it in the records for future reference.
  • Proofread your work to remove grammar issues and words that do not sound right.

    How to write a letter for not attending exam due to illness

    Everyone gets sick unexpectedly once in a while. When you miss an important exam for being absent from school due to illness, writing a letter can go a long way toward fostering goodwill with your principal. Here is a sample of a letter for not attending exam due to illness.

    Date…

    The Principal...

    Name of the School…

    School Address…

    RE: Apology letter for not attending exam

    Dear Sir/Madam,

    With profound regrets, I write this to express sincere apology for my inability to attend the examination held recently on (specify the date). I assure you, however, I couldn't sit for the examination due to circumstances beyond my control. I was suffering from (specify your illness) and due to this, I was not able to come to school, and I also missed my exam.

    Therefore, I request that you excuse me for my inability to sit for the examination. May I also request you to fix another day and time convenient to you to let me make up for the missed exam as the doctor has advised me to go back to school. I shall be really thankful to you for this act of favour.

    How to write application for reappear in exam

    For many students, retakes are an unfamiliar and scary process. However, retakes are an amazing opportunity for you to reach your full potential. Reappearing in exam allow students who are unhappy with their original results to redo the exams in the units they did not perform well.

    Additionally, students whose results are totally missing can also opt for a retake. If you have a compelling reason to write an application letter for reappearing in exam, the following are essential tips to consider.

    1. Tone of voice

    How you write to your friends and loved ones is different from how will write to your principal. When writing to your principal the letter should be formal, and so, the rules that apply when writing an official letter applies to this form of a letter. Hence, make sure that you are polite.

    2. Clarify your reasons why you want to reappear in exam

    You must ensure to clearly state the reasons why you are seeking a retake. You should note that your principal is not likely to approve your request if the reasons are not convincing enough.

    3. Be concise

    When writing this kind of letter, you should be brief and straight to the point. It would be best if you summarise why you need the exam without beating about the bush.

    4. State the number of units you want a retake

    When drafting your letter, after stating your reasons, you also need to state specifically the number of units you would want a retake. With that, the principal can make an informed decision as to whether to approve your request based on your reasons.

    5. Revise after writing

    After you have written your letter, make sure everything is in order. It is advisable to read through again, to ensure that your message is free from any grammar errors. The letter should also have a good flow such that the principal would not have a hard time understanding it.
