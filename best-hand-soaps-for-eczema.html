What is eczema?

“Eczema is a chronic inflammatory skin condition that presents as pink to red-brown rough patches of skin that are often dry, itchy, and irritated,” explains Dr. Libby, adding, “It is believed to be influenced by a combination of genetic, environmental, and immune system factors.”

Why should I use a hand soap for eczema? 

It’s simple: You have a compromised skin barrier that is more likely to experience dryness and irritation. To counteract these side effects, fill your hand soap jar with a formula that’s specially made for eczema. This is going to look like nourishing, hydrating, soothing ingredients that’ll assist with restoring and repairing the skin’s moisture barrier.

What ingredients should I look out for in a hand soap for eczema? 

Colloidal oatmeal, glycerin, hyaluronic acid, ceramides, shea butter, allantoin, and niacinamide are going to become your best friends during your search for the best hand soap for eczema. They’ll help transform your skin from rough, dry, itchy, and inflamed into healthy, smooth, soft, and moisturized.

What ingredients should I avoid in a hand soap for eczema?

Don’t even think about buying a hand soap that contains fragrance, harsh dyes, essential oils, exfoliating beads, or acids like salicylic acid or glycolic acid. These ingredients will likely only worsen your eczema.
