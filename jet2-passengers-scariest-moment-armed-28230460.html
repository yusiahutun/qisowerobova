Jet2 passenger's 'scariest moment' as armed police surround plane amid bomb scare

 Posted on April 5, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  646 words  |  Martina Birk

A terrified passenger has told of her fear after armed police scrambled to a potential bomb threat on her Jet2 flight over Essex.

Jet2 Flight LS922 was on its way from Dalaman in Turkey to Manchester before it suddenly turned around just north of London.

Two RAF planes were scrambled from RAF Coningsby in Lincolnshire just before 8pm, responding to the threat against the Airbus A321.

The aircraft was diverted to Stansted Airport, where police were waiting on standby for the plane to land.

And one of those on board, Andrea Aspinwall, said police had their guns pointed at the aircraft as it sat stationary for around two hours.

She said passengers onboard were told to "sit calm" but that "everyone started panicking".

Andrea, from north Wales, said she sensed that 'something was wrong' when the plane began circling in the moments after the pilot announced that they would soon be landing at Manchester Airport.

Andrea added that they were taken to a 'red point' - a remote stand away from the main passenger terminal - and added: "The air hostesses couldn't tell us what was going on.

"We were sat there and then we saw all these armed police driving up. We were terrified. We knew something was wrong then. Police had their guns pointing at the plane.

"Everyone started panicking, I started crying. I'm disabled so I don't take things good as it is."

Andrea said that passengers found out from social media that there was a bomb threat on the plane. "It was the scariest moment of my life," she said.

Once passengers were allowed off the plane, the wheelchair user said 'the chaos started' as passengers were unsure what would happen next.

She said staff told how they would all be put on a coach to Manchester, but she was put in a hotel at 4.30am.

Martin Ramsay said was onboard the same plane and waited in the airport for 'four hours' to be taken back to Manchester.

Martin tweeted: "@jet2tweets still in Stansted Airport 4 hours later and still about 50 people waiting for transfers to Manchester or local airport.

"Absolutely terrible service from your company. Should do better. Feel glad to be alive after bomb hoax but this is ridiculous. Not good enough."

On Facebook, Kui Unisa wrote: "False alarm everyone. We are all safe and well. We are either staying in a hotel tonight and getting the flight tomorrow OR taxi/train back to Manchester."

After the incident, the RAF confirmed that two Quick Reaction Alert Typhoon aircraft from RAF Coningsby near Horncastle, Lincolnshire, were involved in the response.

Jet2 confirmed that the flight had been diverted at the request of UK authorities due to a "potential security concern".
