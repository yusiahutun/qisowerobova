FAQ

Q: What are gaming gloves for?

Gaming gloves are often used by competitive and professional gamers. Many of them provide an enhanced, anti-slip grip for gamepads and controllers and have an anti-sweat, moisture-wicking design to counteract sweaty palms.

More specialized gloves can provide relief for console and PC gamers who suffer from joint-related issues, providing them with a means to game comfortably for longer.

Q: Why are most gaming gloves fingerless gloves?

With fingerless gaming gloves, players benefit from an enhanced anti-slip grip without any impediment to their rapid finger and thumb movements.

Q: Can gaming gloves help with carpal tunnel?

There are specialized gloves, suitable for gaming, that provide extra compression and can help encourage blood flow. By adding extra compression to your joints, they can help relieve pain and swelling and allow you to play comfortably for longer. These gloves can help players with Carpal Tunnel, as well as RSIs, arthritis, and other joint-related ailments.

Q: Can gaming gloves help with sweaty hands?

Most gaming gloves are made from fabrics that help to wick away palm sweat, helping you to maintain a solid grip on your controller, and minimizing the chances of fumbling the controls.

Q: Do copper gloves help with wrist pain?

Copper compression can assist with joint pain, improving mobility and reducing stiffness. Copper-infused compression gloves act to heat your hands and fingers, which in turn can increase the blood flow and help reduce inflammation around the joints.

Q: How else can I protect my hands and wrists while gaming?

Besides investing in a pair of gaming gloves, you can ease the pressure on your hands and wrists by warming up before gaming and flexing your fingers and wrists before starting. Taking regular breaks will also help alleviate the pressure and keep joints supple.
