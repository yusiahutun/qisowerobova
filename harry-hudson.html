Who is Harry Hudson? 

Harry Hudson is an American singer-songwriter who blends addictive and warm pop vocal melodies with swaggering R&B beats. Hudson, a close associate of Jaden Smith’s MSFTS collective/lifestyle brand, has collaborated with Smith and Astrid S on projects like 2020s Hey, I’m Here for You.

Hudson was born in New Jersey in 1993, and his family relocated to Los Angeles when he was four years old. Throughout his teens, he developed a love for many forms of pop music, like Al Green’s, Michael Jackson’s, and Bruce Springsteen’s work, and eventually decided to pursue songwriting as a career. “World Is Gone,” the singer’s first single, was released online in 2013. However, in June of that year, he was diagnosed with Hodgkin’s cancer and had to put his career on hold while undergoing treatment. In January 2014, he was able to overcome his condition after six months of chemotherapy.

He spent 2015 rediscovering his inspirations and getting to work on his full-length debut, Treatment (A-Side) and Gemineyes, which he released independently in the same year. The formerly included songs he wrote while undergoing chemotherapy, with lyrics mostly centered on his experience, while the latter was co-written with close friend and partner gnash. Hudson then moved to New York, where he developed a passion for movies. Hudson’s own short film, Can Cowboys Cry, co-starred Jaden Smith and featured Hudson’s songs like “Cry for Love” and “Yellow Lights” on its 2017 soundtrack. Yesterday’s Tomorrow Night, his debut album, was released in March 2018 on the MSFTS/Roc Nation label. Hudson returned later that year with “Just Slide,” a collaboration with Jaden Smith, and the groove-heavy “Pendulum” in 2019. Hey, I’m Here for You was released the following year, and it featured the Astrid S duet “Closing Doors.”
