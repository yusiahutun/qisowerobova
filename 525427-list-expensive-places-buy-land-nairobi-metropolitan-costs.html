Land is one of the factors of production, and real estate companies have been making a kill from it.

Hass Consult's land price index quarter two report 2023 revealed land prices in Nairobi’s suburbs stagnated, recording a 0.1% drop in the quarter ending June 2023.

This came as the city faces uncertainty on future demand cycles given the economic slowdown.

The report indicated land prices within satellite towns were also subdued, with minimal increases of just 1.2% in the quarter and 5.1% in the year.

However, four out of the 18 city suburbs tracked- Spring Valley, Donholm, Loresho and Langata- registered double-digit land price increases during the period under review.

Ngong recorded the fastest land increase price of 4.7% in the quarter and 20.7% in the last year.

Sakina Hassanali, the head of development consulting & research at Hass Consult, said land price increases were witnessed in city suburbs with low rigidity of traditionally allowable development densities.

"Land pricing is a function of its development potential. Therefore, land in areas with strict development density guidelines tends to have a price ceiling while land in areas that are subject to less rigidity around the densities tends to increase in pricing in tandem with the approved density," Hassanali said.

Which are expensive places to buy land in Nairobi?

1. Upper Hill

Hass Consult noted land in the Upper Hill area is the most expensive in the city.

The average cost of an acre in the area is KSh 478.7 million. Upper Hill is one of the city's commercial hubs, hosting local and international companies.

2. Westlands

Westlands has the second-most expensive pieces of land in the Nairobi Metropolitan region.

To buy an acre of land in Westlands, one part with at least KSh 452.4 million. Land value in Westlands increased by 3.2% in the last year.

3. Parklands

Parklands near Westlands was ranked third, with an acre costing approximately KSh 403.9 million.

During the quarter, land prices in the area declined by 0.4%. However, the prices rose by 2.4% in the year to June 2023.

4. Kilimani

Kilimani is another suburb with prime land in Nairobi city.

The Hass Consult index indicated an acre in Kilimani retails at KSh 396.9 million, a drop of 1.7% compared to a similar period in 2022.

Kilimani was once considered a serene environment before landlords built highrise residential apartments.

5. Riverside

Riverside completes the list of the top five most expensive suburbs to buy land in the city.

An acre of land in the area costs KSh 320.9 million. Some wealthy politicians who own property there are Prime Cabinet Secretary Musalia Mudavadi and former Nairobi governor Evans Kidero.

Which are the most expensive estates in Nairobi?

In related news, the Nairobi Metropolitan has some of the most expensive estates in Africa.

Gigiri suburb is home to the most expensive houses in Nairobi, while Kitengela was placed as the least expensive neighbourhood.

A typical four-bedroom villa in Nyari estate costs between KSh 200,000 and KSh 300,000, while house prices average KSh 103.6 million.

To own a house in Runda, you must part with at least KSh 92.1 million, whereas monthly rent costs between KSh 200,000 and KSh 300,000.
