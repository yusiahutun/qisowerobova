NTSA portal: account registration and vehicle search

 Posted on March 26, 2024  |  13 minutes  |  2559 words  |  Martina Birk

Have you heard of the National Transport and Safety Authority (NTSA)? Do you know its mandate in Kenya? NTSA is a statutory body established to bring about sanity on our roads. It brings together under one roof the roles of motor vehicle registration, transport licensing and motor vehicle inspection, road safety, driver testing and to some extent, traffic law enhancement. Read here for more on how to register on NTSA portal to receive NTSA services quickly and with utmost convenience.

The National Transport and Safety Authority (NTSA) introduced a new online platform-Transport Integrated Management System (TIMS) where you can register and transfer your motor vehicle. NTSA TIMS was launched at the end of 2016 and had initially been available to car dealers. If you are selling or buying a motor vehicle, you will need an NTSA TIMS account. This is because NTSA declared that all motor vehicle transfer would be done online. This requires both the seller and the buyer to have an account on the NTSA portal.

Core functions of NTSA

Main functions of the Authority as outlined in section 4 of the NTSA Act are: -

  • To advise and make recommendations on matters relating to road transport and safety,
  • To implement policies relating to road transport and safety
  • To plan, manage and regulate the road transport sector in accordance with the provisions of the Act, and
  • To ensure the provision of safe, reliable and efficient road transport service.

Mandate of NTSA

  • Register and license motor vehicles,
  • Conduct motor vehicle inspection and certification,
  • Regulate public service vehicles,
  • Advise the Government on national policy with regard to road transport sector,
  • Develop and implement road safety strategies,
  • Facilitate the education of members of the public on road safety,
  • Conduct research and audits on road safety,
  • Compile inspection reports relating to traffic accidents,
  • Establish systems and procedures for, and oversee the training, testing and licensing of drivers,
  • Formulate and review the curriculum of driving schools, and coordinate the activities of persons and organizations dealing in matters relating to road safety.

NTSA TIMS registration

NTSA TIMS allows people to transfer vehicles and to conduct a number plate search at their convenience through their TIMS account

Follow the steps below to create an account and login to the NTSA portal.

  • Open the NTSA TIMS portal
  • Click on 'Register an Account' in the upper right corner to enter the 'register an account' interface
  • Click on the 'individuals' icon and request verification of individual’s ID Serial No.
  • Verify the user’s mother’s maiden name (for individuals)
  • Verify individual PIN number. Two buttons will appear below after passing verification. Click next to continue.
  • After verification of the email verification code, enter Kenyan phone number in the correct format in the mobile phone number box, click on the 'verify phone' button to verify the phone number, then send an authorization code button appears under 'mobile phone number' box. If the phone number has not been subscribed with NTSA SMS, it will prompt SMS has not been subscribed to. If the phone number has been subscribed with SMS, click 'send authorization code' button and the system will send the code to the mobile phone.
  • Click on the 'next' button to submit registration information, it will show successful registration.

    How to search online for the registered owner of a motor vehicle

    Thanks to the NTSA portal you can now search online to determine who owns a vehicle as per the government records. This is very important especially when you intend to purchase a second-hand vehicle. Below are the steps to conducting the search;

  • Log on to your e-citizen account. If you do not have any, create one on registration page of e-citizen.
  • Once your account is set up or if you already have an account log on to e-citizen.
  • Select National Transport Safety Authority and click “get service now”
  • Continue to select “make an application”
  • Then select “motor vehicle registration”
  • Select “motor vehicle search (copy of records)
  • Choose “motor vehicle search (copy of records)”
  • Click on apply now and enter the vehicle registration number without space, e.g. KCA787A
  • Select your mode of payment and follow the instructions on the website to download the requested registration details.

    How to transfer vehicle ownership, logbook online

    NTSA logbook transfer has been made much easier and convenient by the introduction of the TIMS portal. Below are the steps on how you can transfer ownership of your vehicle online. This comes in handy especially when buying or selling your car. The transfer materializes within seven days.

    Register for NTSA TIMS account- both parties should have a TIM account. You will need your ID card so that you can refer to your ID’s serial number (not the ID card number). Follow the prompts to ensure that registration has been successful and that your account has been created. You will receive an SMS on your phone giving the password. That is what you use to log in the first time.

    Log in to TIMS through ntsa.go.ke. As part of the login process, you will receive a security code on your phone which you will be required to feed in on the TIMS system for verification.

    Transfer of a vehicle -the seller

  • To access the transfer menu click on the Vehicle registration (1) button
  • Click on “apply for transfer of ownership
  • Continue and select on “create new button”
  • Type the registration number of the vehicle you want to transfer.
  • Then click on the inquire button. This page shows the basic information of the vehicle to be transferred including the original owner.
  • At this point, click “view” to see the particulars of the vehicle and upload a scanned copy of the logbook. Under “new vehicle owners” the seller should select individual/non individual/financier then enter buyers’ details. The buyer can be either an individual, company of a financial institution. In case of individuals enter the National ID and use PIN number for companies/financial institutions.
  • Move on and click the 'add' button.
  • Then go to security verification and select mobile. Click send verification code and feed in the code sent to your mobile number.
  • Tick on disclaimer displayed.

    Transfer of a vehicle- the buyer

  • Once the seller clicks on submit, the buyer receives a notification to inform them that the vehicle has been transferred and they are required to accept ownership.
  • To accept/reject the transfer, the buyer will have to log in to the NTSA portal.
  • Secondly, click on 'accept ownership' and key in the registration number. Then click on 'Inquire'.
  • Click “view” to see the details of the vehicle.
  • Under “security verification”, select the verification mode, in this case, mobile. Then click send a verification code.
  • The system will send a verification code to the buyer’s mobile phone.
  • Fill in the verification code sent to your mobile phone into the field and click 'Accept' or 'Decline' on the transfer.
  • Once you select 'decline', the vehicle reverts to the seller.
  • Once you 'accept', you are required to fill out all the relevant information required..
  • Then, click 'submit' for the application to be forwarded to NTSA for processing and issuance of logbook within seven working days.

    Driving license requirements

    The following conditions should be met for one to acquire a driving license. A licensing officer will not grant a driving license to applicants who do not meet the laid down conditions. The applicant should satisfy the licensing officer that:

    • He/she has passed a competency test to drive the particular class of vehicle and possesses a certificate of competence
    • He/she holds a valid driving license for the class of vehicle that has been granted by a credible authority in a commonwealth country
    • He/she holds an international driving permit
    • The applicant should make a declaration on whether he/she suffers from a disease or physical disability that may make his driving of a motor vehicle to be dangerous to the public.
    • The applicant should be able to read with or without glasses, a vehicle number plate from a distance of twenty-five meters. Partially/totally blind applicants are not allowed to hold a driving license

    How to renew your driving license

  • Begin with login into the NYSA portal and sign up to get your TIMS account. You will be required to give your identity card number and your first name as it appears on the ID.
  • After creating the account, the applicants are redirected to a new window which displays the services rendered on the portal. The services include applications for a provisional driving license, a driving test, and interim driving license. Other services include duplicate driving license, driving class endorsement, corrections on driving license information. Select the driving license renewal option.
  • After entering this interface, you will receive guidelines for the whole process. You are required to provide the Drivers’ license reference number that appears on the first page of the license. A fee old 650 shillings will be charged per year and 1450 for three years.
  • Once you are done, click “apply now”.
  • A new interface appears asking you to fill in your Driving license reference number as well as the renewal period. Select the mode of payment, either mPesa/Airtel money or debit/credit card.
  • Once you are done with the payment, submission of details and printing of the renewal slip, you will be good to go.

    NTSA inspection booking in Kenya

    Booking for inspection was a lot of work in the past. Walking around with paper around the government offices was the order of the day. NTSA inspection booking is now available both manually and online. It can be either annual, for a change of ownership or even for a new car. This service is now a walk in the park, and all you need to access is the TIMS portal to book your appointments.

    Here are the steps to follow when booking an inspection appointment.

  • Go to the NTSA website.
  • You need to create a TIMS account if you do not have one and fill in the required information.
  • Click on vehicle inspection tab and select the vehicle that you want to be inspected.
  • Choose whether it is annual, change the particulars of the vehicle or new vehicle inspection and then click next.
  • Continue to make your payment via the modes of payments available and proceed to print the invoice.
  • Then click the back tab and then on the booking button.
  • Select the inspection station that is closest to you.
  • Choose the ideal time for you among the options provided in the system.
  • Print the inspection booking slip and thereafter take the vehicle inspection Kenya at the scheduled time.
