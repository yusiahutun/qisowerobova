Big Brother, little audience

The revamped celebrity version of Big Brother flopped on its first night, attracting less than half the audience of last year.

Big Brother: Celebrity Hijack drew 3.4million viewers to Channel 4 and E4 on Thursday, compared to 7.1million for Celebrity Big Brother in 2007.

Channel 4 bosses had altered the format after last year's race row between Shilpa Shetty, Jade Goody and Danielle Lloyd.

This year the 'celebrities' take a presenting role. They will act as Big Brother, tormenting 12 young adults chosen for their intelligence and talent. Little Britain's Matt Lucas was the first to become Big Brother.

The programme was simultaneously broadcast on Channel 4 and E4 from 9pm to 10pm.

The last half hour, which was broadcast on E4 only, drew

1.9million viewers - the digital channel's highest-ever audience for a home-grown commission. From today, the programme will be shown on E4 only.

A spokesman for the broadcaster said it was happy with the ratings, adding: 'The figures are not really comparable with Celebrity Big Brother because this is an E4 show.

'It is completely different programme, from the format to the selection of the housemates, and it is geared towards a younger audience.'

Scottish Youth Parliament chairman John Loughton is the early favourite to win the £50,000 prize, after completing a task set by Lucas.

Future celebrities to play Big Brother include the comedian Jimmy Carr and Joan Rivers.
