Video shows Guatemalan mum punish son in Escuintla by standing on him

 Posted on August 2, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  433 words  |  Larita Shotwell

This child may never misbehave again, but he could also be scarred for life.

An alarming video from Guatemala shows the poor boy's mother standing on top of him while she chastises him outside in the rain.

The footage is going viral in South America and has sparked outrage on social media.

An alarming video from Guatemala shows a poor boy's mother standing on top of him as a punishment

He can only cry out for help when she lifts off her foot, near the southern-central city of Escuintla

In the film it is not clear why the woman dishes out the harsh punishment to her child, reported to be just six years of age.

Guatemalan news site Prensa Libre reports the shocking scene occurred at the camp La Barrita, near the southern-central city of Escuintla.

The woman pressed her right foot down on the small child's chest, and his cries could only be heard when she lifted it off to allow him to breathe.

She then violently dragged him by the arm across the hard ground.

The news channel that shared the images posted the message of an angry sender.

It is not clear why the woman dishes out the harsh punishment to her child, reported to be just six years of age

 The angry mother then violently drags the boy by the arm across the hard ground outside in the rain

It read: 'Hello, I want this video to go viral so that this lady who abuses her son can be stopped. 

'She lives in a community in the neighbourhood of San Jose Escuintla. I want them to do it anonymously, but please soon, put an end to the violence.' 

The State Attorney’s Office for Human Rights (PDH) shared the video on their Twitter page, asking anyone with information to come forward.

Lesbia Salguero, a spokesperson for the PDH, said investigators went to the location where the punishment took place but believed the mother was alerted and fled.

They claim the aggressor is somewhere in La Gomera, Escuintla.

On social media almost all were outraged at the scene.

Roxana Marroquin wrote: 'We all have ways to control our children and sometimes we get frustrated or there are problems, but that's no way to beat them.'
