What to book: Prt--Portea at The Berkeley

 Posted on August 10, 2024  |  1 minutes  |  210 words  |  Aldo Pusey

There are few things more quintessentially English than afternoon tea, so for the Queen's upcoming 90th birthday it seems a fitting tribute to indulge in her honour.

From 18 to 24 April, to tie in with Her Majesty's birthday on 21 April, the Berkeley will be putting a royal twist on their fashion-themed afternoon-tea menu, with treats inspired by a number of regal accessories. A vanilla biscuit becomes Queen Elizabeth's Coronation crown, complete with edible diamonds, and a blueberry cake takes inspiration from a hat designed by Rachel Trevor-Morgan, one of the Queen's favourite British milliners.

However, our favourite tea-stand treat has to be the praline profiterole in the shape of the controversial Philip Treacy fascinator worn by Princess Beatrice to the wedding of the Duke and Duchess of Cambridge. The headpiece may have received mixed reviews, but the cream-filled pastry is unarguably a delight.

Prêt-à-Portea is served at the Berkeley from 1:45pm – 5:30pm Monday – Friday and 1:00pm - 5:30pm on Friday - Sunday priced at £45 per person (£52 including a glass of Laurent Perrier champagne or £58 per person including a glass of couture champagne). To make a reservation, call 0207 107 8866. The Berkeley, Wilton Place, London SW1X 7RL. www.the-berkeley.co.uk
