8 Easter Family Photoshoot Ideas to Try in 2023

 Posted on March 21, 2024  |  5 minutes  |  1024 words  |  Larita Shotwell

Do you know what else is around the corner when spring comes? It is Easter—a time for celebrating with friends and family. Easter is especially fun for kids who can run around and enjoy the great outdoors without all the heavy winter wear weighing them down. It is also time to see all the new life bloom around them.

What better way to capture the season's essence than with a family photoshoot? Here are some ideas to make your Easter photo session super fun and memorable.

1. Add Some Bunny Ears

It's an Easter photoshoot, so you must have something related to bunnies. If you're okay with extensive planning, order bunny costumes for everyone in the family. Want to keep things simple? Then get some bunny ears. They are inexpensive and available everywhere.

Try different locations for your photoshoot. You have many options, from your living room to your backyard and neighborhood parks. You can use a tripod and put your camera on self-timer mode if you want to be in the photo. Remember to keep your aperture around f/4 or more to have everyone in focus.

2. Enjoy the Spring Outdoors

After months of seeing the world in white and grey, it is a welcome sight to see all the colors that spring brings. So, don't miss the opportunity to spend some time outdoors and fit in a casual photo session there.

Spring is also when the famous cherry blossom happens in many cities. You can get a gorgeous pink floral background if you plan your shoot around this time.

Choose cloudy days so that you can get a naturally diffused light that will bring out the rich colors. You can still shoot on hot days, but make sure to find a shade and carry a collapsible diffuser. Editing is also crucial to make your spring photos come alive.

3. Baby in a Bird's Nest

If you have a new member in your family, all the more reason to celebrate Easter and have a photo session. You can use a shallow wicker or rattan basket for a makeshift nest. You can also use a grapevine wreath to get a more natural look. Line the basket with a cozy blanket, and your custom nest is ready for your little one.

You can also add fun props like a soft bird toy or colorful eggs. If your baby is a newborn, ensure they are comfortable and supported well.

Do you own a macro lens, or can your smartphone shoot in macro mode? If yes, you can get closer and capture the details, like your baby's feet and hands.

4. Capture an Easter Egg Hunt

You can't celebrate Easter without an egg hunt. So capture the action when your kids are hunting for the eggs. Prepare them in advance to wear complementary clothing, find some natural baskets, and remove any unwanted objects in the background.

The key thing is to let your kids be natural and snap their genuine expressions. You can compose your scene creatively, but trying to pose them will give you only forced shots. Also, step back and include details in the background to tell the story effectively.

Keep the shutter speed high so you can capture the movement without getting blurry images.

5. Include Real Animals

The idea of including live animals may sound scary, but it is not that hard. One place where you can find friendly animals is on a farm. So, visit a farm nearby and check if they can let you use the tame animals in your pictures.

You may also have to consider your child's temperament before planning a shoot with the animals. For example, bunnies, chicks, and lambs are not likely to pose any serious threat but still ensure all parties involved are comfortable with each other.

It's a good idea to plan the shoot in the morning, during the golden hour, when you can get lovely warm light. Use a shallow depth of field to get a creamy, blurred background. Avoid using flash, which can bother both animals and kids.

6. Catch the Action in the Kitchen

The heart of the family is the kitchen, so take your camera there to get some authentic pictures. Ask your family to move the baking action near a window, so you can get ample light to work with. You can use artificial lighting if your kitchen doesn't get enough sunlight.

You're not taking pictures for a menu card, so embrace the mess and include them in your photos. For example, flour scattered all over with fruit splashes around will make your photos look more natural and in the moment.

7. Go on a Family Picnic

You and your family can take advantage of the warmer weather and enjoy a picnic. Bring your colorful picnic mat, and remember to showcase the seasonal vegetables in your spring picnic photos.

Along with the food and accessories, don't forget the capture the emotions of your family members, including your pets. Again, use a tripod if you want to be in the images.

You can also coordinate your clothing; soft pastels fit perfectly with the season's colors.

8. Adorn With Floral Crowns

This can be a fun DIY project for your kids. You can find wildflowers or buy your favorite ones from the shop and make floral crowns for the whole family. You can take portraits of each family member individually and then with the siblings together.

Get some behind the scene pictures, too—collecting wildflowers, working on the flower crown, trying it on each other, and so on can be fun memories to keep.

You don't need a DSLR to take great pictures; your smartphone camera can be handy for quick photoshoots like this.

Make Memories Together This Easter

We have only covered a few Easter photoshoot ideas. We are sure you can come up with a much more extensive list of photoshoot ideas. So get your family outside and make some memories as the weather warms up. Grab your camera along the way, and capture those memories.

Better still, print your photos and create a scrapbook or album to cherish forever.
