If you’re a fan of movies that blend humor, heart, and a touch of magical realism, then you’re in for a treat with the film “Local Hero.” Released in 1983, this British comedy-drama directed by Bill Forsyth has earned a special place in the hearts of movie lovers around the world. Set in the picturesque Scottish village of Ferness, “Local Hero” follows the story of an ambitious American oil company executive, Mac MacIntyre, who is sent to secure land for a new refinery. The movie Local Hero was released in 1983.

Set against the beautiful backdrop of the Scottish Highlands, this film tells the story of an American oil executive sent to buy an entire village as a location for a new refinery.

The screenplay was written by Bill Forsyth.

Bill Forsyth not only directed Local Hero but also penned its screenplay. His unique vision and storytelling skills brought the characters and setting to life.

The film features a brilliant ensemble cast.

Local Hero boasts a talented cast including Peter Riegert, Peter Capaldi, Burt Lancaster, Denis Lawson, and Jenny Seagrove. Each actor brings their own charm and charisma to their respective roles.

It explores themes of greed and environmentalism.

The movie delves into the clash between corporate greed and the preservation of nature, as the villagers of Ferness resist selling their land for industrial development.

The fictional village of Ferness is actually Pennan in Aberdeenshire.

Pennan, a real village in Scotland, served as the main filming location for Ferness in the movie. Its breathtaking coastal scenery captivates viewers throughout the film.

The film received positive reviews from critics.

Local Hero was praised for its humor, heartwarming story, and beautiful cinematography. It successfully blended comedy and drama, leaving a lasting impact on audiences.

The soundtrack was composed by Mark Knopfler.

The iconic music of Local Hero was composed by Mark Knopfler, the lead guitarist and vocalist of the rock band Dire Straits. The score perfectly captures the spirit of the film.
