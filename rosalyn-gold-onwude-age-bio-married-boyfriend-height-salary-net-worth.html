Rosalyn Gold-Onwude’s Bio, Wiki

Rosalyn Gold-Onwude was born on 28 April 1987 in Queens, New York, the United States. Her mother, Pat Gold comes from the Russian and Jewish descent, whereas, her father, Austin Onwude is of Nigerian. Similarly, she holds an American nationality and belongs to the mixed ethnicity.

Since her early age, Onwude was so much active in the sports field, especially in basketball. During her high school at Archbishop Molly High School in Briarwood, New York. Her school team won two state titles in 2003-04. During the time, she also faced a lot of injuries which made her unable to participate in the senior season.

Sports Career

After making a huge name in her high school and being Molly’s second all-time leading scorer in basketball, Onwude went to accept a scholarship to Stanford University where she later worked as a starting point guard for two years under Tara VanDerveer, a Stanford basketball coach.

As she was still suffering from her second knee injury, the pain didn’t allow her to play in the 2006-07 season. However, her injury didn’t last for a long and she made her comeback as a shooting guard in the season 2007-08.

In the final season, Rosa was honored as the Pac-10 Defensive Player of the Year. Later, she went to broaden her experience at the University of Albany, SUNY.

Broadcasting Career

After getting a degree with a BA in communications and a master’s degree in sociology, Rosalyn worked for Tesla Motors for a short time and later pursued her career as a sports broadcaster. Currently, she is managing her time for TNT, Pac-12 Network and MSG. She also analyzes the WNBA’s New York Liberty.

Not to mention, she has also served for the Golden State Warriors during the NBA championship season 2014-15. Apart from her career, she manages time for playing basketball. She has also made her father proud of her as she has already gone to play for the Nigerian team.

Once she mentioned:

“As a woman of color in sports broadcasting, I want to do good work and have a positive, visible influence”; and, “I hope other young women will look at what I’m doing and realize they too could have a career in sports media if they desire.”

Currently, Gold is serving the TNT and NBA-TV as she announced her retirement from NBC Sports Bay Area on 1st September 2017.
