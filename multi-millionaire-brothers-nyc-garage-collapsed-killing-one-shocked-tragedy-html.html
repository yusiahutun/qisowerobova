Multi-millionaire brothers who own NYC garage that collapsed killing one are 'shocked' by tragedy

 Posted on March 19, 2024  |  7 minutes  |  1434 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

Multi-millionaire brothers who own the New York City garage which collapsed and killed the building manager are 'shocked' by the tragedy, DailyMail.com can reveal.

Jeffrey and Alan Henick jointly own the property on Ann Street in the Financial District, which collapsed at 4pm on Tuesday.

Veteran employee Willis Moore, 59, was killed in the incident with five others also being injured – as authorities continue to probe the cause of the collapse.

The garage is listed as being owned by 57 Ann Street Realty Associates, which is registered to the brothers, according to public records.

A source close to the family confirmed to DailyMail.com that they were aware of the incident and were 'shocked' by what had happened.

New York City's Department of Buildings confirmed that there are four open property violations relating to the garage in Lower Manhattan – with two referring to 'cracked concrete'.

57 Ann St Realty Association was fined a total of $1,150 in 2003 because of two of the complaints, but it does not appear that anything was resolved before the incident on Tuesday.

The building has been owned by the Henicks since at least 1988, the earliest posted record in the city's property database. 

They also own Long Island-based Western Carpet and Linoleum, with the brothers taking over the family business after the death of their father Murray in 2014.
