Cheapest private universities in Nigeria in 2024

In Nigeria, some private universities are for the rich. Many parents struggle to raise the fees, yet schools keep frustrating them by raising tuition fees. Luckily, some private universities are affordable and do not strain the pocket. Below are the cheapest universities according to Bscholarly:

1. Oduduwa University Ipetumodu (N98,000 - N35,000)

Oduduwa University Ipetumodu is located in Ipetumodu Ile-Ife, Osun state. It is one of the cheapest private universities in Nigeria in 2024. The school offers affordable tuition fees, quality education, and arange of opportunities for aspiring students.

It serves as a testament to the notion that affordable education can be accessible without compromising excellence. This institution is named after the progenitor of the Yorba race, Oduduwa. It is accredited and recognized by the National Universities Commission of Nigeria.

This institution offers financial help and scholarships to underprivileged deserving students who wish to attend this university. However, students are required to pay for books every semester and their meals. The fee at Oduduwa University Ipetumodu is between N98,000 to N35,000.

2. Ajayi Crowther University (N250,000 - N950,000)

Ajayi Crowther was developed in 2005 and has since been raising God-fearing scholars. This institution is located in Oyo state. Ajayi Crowther University offers diverse undergraduate programmes.

Ajayi is known to be one of the best institutions in Nigeria. This university has flexible paying schedules, and paying in instalments is allowed. The hostels at Ajayi Crowther University are comfortable and fairly cheap.

Recreational facilities at the university are also available. However, students are required to pay for their books and recreational facilities. This university's fee is between N250,000 to N950,000.

3. Al-Hikmah University (N146,000 - N856,000)

Al-Hikmah University is a Muslim university. If you are looking for an affordable Muslim university, this is definitely for you. It is located in Ilorin, Kwara state. The school was started in 2005 with only 70 students. Over the years, it has grown and now has over 7000 students.

Al-Hikmah University also offers flexible payment plans, and students can pay the fees in two to three instalments. This university also offers financial help and scholarships to those who wish to study there. Al-Hikmah University fee is between N146,000 to N856,000.

4. Igbinedion University (N320,000 - N460,000)

Igbinedion University was founded in 1999 and is located in Okada. It is one of the cheapest private universities in Nigeria that offers medicine and surgery. The school provides a dynamic, stimulating, diverse research teaching environment supported by standard facilities. It is also a private university in Nigeria without a JAMB. The fee at Igbinedion University is between N320,000 to N460,000.

5. Adeleke University (N250,000 - N1500,000)

Adeleke University was founded by Dr. Adedeji Adeleke, the father of Davido (a famous Afrobeat musician). It is known for its high-quality education. Adeleke University was founded in 2011 via the Springtime Development Foundation (SDF).

Adeleke University is a faith-based institution based on the Seventh-day Adventist philosophy of education. It is located in Ede, Osun state. It offers loans and scholarships to its students who cannot afford the fees.

This university offers innovative learning and the acquisition of entrepreneurial skills. The fees at Adeleke University are between N250,000 to N1500,000.

6. Lead City University (N250,000 - N160,000)

Lead City is located in Ibadan, Oyo state. It is one of the best accredited private universities in the area. It is mostly known for its strict rules, which every student should comply with; failure to which a student risks suspension.

This school has great amenities like modern computer facilities and an electronic library. A student must pay for the above, but they are fairly cheap compared to other private universities. The fees at Lead City University are between N250,000 and N160,000.

7. Caritas University (N170,000 - N390,000)

Caritas University was authorised as a private university on December 16 2004. On January 31, 2005, Prof Fabian Osuji, the Federal Minister for Education at the time, gave the university its formal opening. Caritas University is located in Moji-Nike, Enugu state.

It offers its students a flexible payment plan, and they can pay their fees in two to three instalments. Caritas University also offers loans and scholarships to its underprivileged students. The fees at Caritas University are between N170,000 to N390,000.

Which is the cheapest private university in Abuja? Veritas University is one of the cheapest Universities in Abuja. It was established in 2009 and has become one of Nigeria's most luxurious top-ranking universities today.

9. Paul University (N190,000 - N240,000)

Paul University is the cheapest private university in Lagos. It is affiliated to the Anglican Church and was established in 2009. It is located in Awka, Anambra state. This university offers a conducive environment for learning and growing mentally and professionally.

It offers a flexible payment plan to its students. Loans and scholarships are also offered at Paul University to underprivileged students. This is one of the most affordable private universities in Nigeria. Its fees range between N190,000 and N240,000.

11. Obong University (N170,000)

Obong University is built on a foundation of Christian values, and a commitment to academic excellence and character development. The institution, located in the quiet country town of Obong Ntak, Akwa Ibom State, was established in 2007 to offer affordable access to quality education.

The university boasts a conducive environment for holistic learning and offers programs in disciplines such as social, management, and natural sciences. With a starting fee of N170,000. Obong University is one of the most affordable private universities in the country.
