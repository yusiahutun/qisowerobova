Frank Mason III Bio

Frank Mason was born on April 3, 1994, in Petersburg, Virginia. He grew up with his large family in Petersburg. His father was Frank Mason II and his mother was Sharon Harrison. They moved to Pin Oaks, Petersburg.

Mason attended St. Petersburg High School and there he began to make a real impression on the court. His total score in four years of 1,901 points is only surpassed in school history by the NBA Hall of Famer Moses Malone. His trip to college was a bit tricky as he failed in a government class that prevented him from going to Towson University in Maryland.

Mason attended the Massanutten Military Academy in Woodstock, Virginia, to make up for his failed class, but he was soon offered a scholarship from the University of Kansas after he was discovered by Kurtis Townsend, the assistant coach of Kansas, who played in the Amateur Athletic Union.

In his first year, he averaged 5.5 points, 2.1 assists, and 1.3 rebounds per game, but in his second year, Mason had significantly improved these scores with an average of 12.6 points, 3.9 assists, and 3.9 rebounds per game. He was also named to the second team of the All-Big 12.
