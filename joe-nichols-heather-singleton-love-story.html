How Joe &amp; Heather Nichols Survived 5 Devastating Miscarriagesand Came Out Stronger on the Other S

 Posted on April 28, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  829 words  |  Martina Birk

Some love stories begin in an instant. Others unfold gradually over time and take detours along the way. Take country star Joe Nichols and his wife, Heather Singleton Nichols, who met when they were teenagers but didn't marry until 12 years later.

In honor of Joe's new album, "Never Gets Old," and the couple's upcoming 10-year anniversary, we're looking back on their timeless love story—and the tragic losses they suffered along the way to happily ever after.

"Sometimes, it feels like we're just 21, and I've barely begun lovin' you."

"Sometimes, it feels like we're just 21, and I've barely begun lovin' you," goes the chorus of Joe's recent single. The sweet lyrics are seemingly inspired by his own personal life, even though the then-aspiring artist was really just18 when he first met Heather in 1995. He was singing at Rio Palm Isle in Longview, Texas—which just so happened to be her father's club. But college and Joe's music put their relationship on hold, and it wasn't until September 9, 2007 that the two tied the knot, both at the age of 30.

"The timing was always wrong, but in hindsight, it was probably right, because we weren't ready then," the bride told People prior to the wedding ceremony at Bethesda School for Boys in Savannah, Georgia. "Now is the right time. I'm so sure, I'm not even nervous." Joe, too, said he felt like "the happiest man in the world," calling Heather "the love of my life."

By then, Joe had No. 1 songs like "Tequila Makes Her Clothes Fall Off," and "Brokenheartsville" under his belt—with more hits including "Gimme That Girl" on the way.

Everything seemed pretty perfect at first. "It was so romantic," Heather said to People. "Then came the shock." Just one month after their wedding, Joe checked into rehab for alcoholism. Heather reportedly had no idea about his drinking problem. "He put so much energy into hiding it," she said. "It was complete devastation. Besides being sad, I had a lot of anger, like, 'How could you have done this to me?'" Heather had serious doubts about Joe and their marriage, but her dad reminded her that Joe needed her now more than ever.

"We faced this ugly thing 30 days after our wedding. I felt like if we could get through that, that's a good sign."

"[Joe] told me, 'In you, I see who I am,'" remembered Heather. "It really bonded us. We faced this ugly thing 30 days after our wedding. I felt like if we could get through that, that's a good sign."

The worst seemed to be behind them, but they had no idea the challenges they had yet to face. In a 2012 blog post, Heather revealed that they had been struggling to have a baby, suffering five miscarriages in three years.

"Growing up, out of all of my friends I was the one that didn't care about college or a high-powered job," she wrote. "All I ever wanted was children. My mother and my sisters all had babies so easily that it never crossed my mind that I would have any trouble."

Gradually, doctors began to understand more and more about why Heather kept miscarrying, including her body's "autoimmune response to pregnancy" and a thyroid condition. When Heather lost the fifth baby, she nearly lost hope as well.

"At this 9 week appointment, we saw a beautiful baby but no heartbeat. My husband and I were devastated. This was my lowest point I think because I was doing everything I could do. My stomach was black and blue from the heparin shots, my arms were sore from the constant bloodwork, but my heart was in the worse shape of all. If this news wasn't bad enough I had to do a D&C because the pregnancy like my first one had gone too far to pass naturally. After the surgery I felt robbed. My baby was gone along with my spirit."

Finally, in 2012, after undergoing IVF, IVIG, steroid therapy, and unimaginable emotional pain, the couple welcomed a healthy baby girl, whom they named Dylan River in honor of Heather's childhood friend who passed away.

"This is one of the most humbling and remarkable experiences," Joe told People. "Heather is amazing, and I am just so thankful our little girl is here. She is truly a miracle."

Another miracle followed in 2014. Joe and Heather named their second daughter together Georgia Blue. Suddenly, Joe was surrounded by girls (he also has a daughter named Ashelyn, now 19, from a previous relationship).

"I enjoy all the pink everywhere, being Heather's husband and the daddy for the girls," he told People. "I'll do anything to make them happy."

After all they've been through, Joe told GAC shortly after Dylan was born, "There's no way Heather should still be married to me. There's no way. It's only by grace."
