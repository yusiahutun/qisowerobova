LaLiga: Stuani is Boca Juniors' Plan B

 Posted on July 25, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  354 words  |  Larita Shotwell

Boca Juniors have lined up Tigres striker Andre-Pierre Gignac as their ideal replacement for Dario Benedetto, who is on the verge of joining Marseille, though Los Xeneizes have made Cristhian Stuani their Plan B option if they fail to sign the Frenchman.

Although Gignac himself is keen on the move, Tigres will not roll over and Boca need to have alternative targets. The idea of bringing Stuani to La Bombonera is possible, too, particularly following Girona's relegation to LaLiga SmartBank.

The Uruguayan striker has been in Europe for the last 10 years and has been with Girona since the summer of 2017, with the Catalan side's sporting director, Quique Carcel, explaining Stuani's situation after he returned for pre-season earlier this week. "He is happy in Girona, I know that there are teams that wanted to pay his release clause, but he has rejected them," Carcel said.

Stuani came through at Danubio in Uruguay before making the leap to Europe in 2008 as he joined Reggina. He would then go on to play in Spain for seven years, featuring for the likes of Albacete, Levante and Racing Santander, but it was his performances at Espanyol that earned him a move to the Premier League with Middlesbrough, where he spent two seasons.

However, he took his game to another level at Girona as he played as an out-and-out No.9, rather than an unorthodox winger like he did at Espanyol. In the 2017/18 season he scored 21 goals in 31 games, and last season he found the back of the net on 19 occasions in 29 games, accumulating 40 goals in 62 matches. As a result, Stuani has been a regular name in Uruguay's squad lists for the major competitions, such as the last two Copa Americas and the 2018 World Cup.

"It was Stuani himself who rejected the proposals from the teams that wanted to pay his release clause," Carcel stated at a press conference. "So far he hasn't made a decision to leave." To secure his signature, Boca would need to pay a figure close to six million euros, however their priority is Gignac.
