Beyonc Accused Of Extreme Witchcraft By Former Drummer

 Posted on July 1, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  282 words  |  Larita Shotwell

(CelebrityAccess) A former drummer for Beyoncé has filed a lawsuit against her, accusing Queen Bey of using “extreme witchcraft” as a form of harassment.

Kimberly Thompson, who played with Beyoncé in the all-girl Sugar Mama, wants a restraining order because the performer is using her spells to run surveillance and control her finances, according to a court filing obtained by The Blast.

“I worked for her as her drummer for her band for 7 years,” Thompson claims and, for some reason, Bey started using “extreme witchcraft, dark magic” and “magic spells of sexual molestation” against Thompson, tapped her phone and even murdered Thompson’s kitten.

Thompson released several albums on her own label and played with the 8G Band on “Late Night with Seth Meyers,” according to The Blast.

DJ Jon Quick of New York’s WBLS said Thompson was a “former artistic partner” and suggested she was struggling with mental illness. Her Twitter account, as noted by Heavy.com, is very woo-woo, with posts about “God, divinity, goddesses and about turning away from evil and unkind people.” Recent YouTube uploads have her reading Psalms and playing acoustic guitar. She also has a “kids series” of music on YouTube.

“Healthy living can become contagious if we all do our own individual part in the practice of ‘Self Love.’ Awareness of the properties we expedite can substantially validate the changes we need to make to prolong our lives as a species. Daily walking and running, and pure education on our food and drinks to eliminate waste from parasitic ailments. The Sun is a HUGE healing factor providing safety with in the Temple of God,” she wrote.

Thompson’s request for a temporary restraining order was denied.
