Even 'ugly schmucks' need love: dating apps for people seeking everything from clowns to mullets

 Posted on January 1, 0001  |  8 minutes  |  1672 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

The heart wants what it wants — whether it's a special someone who gets along with your friends and family, or a majestic and timeless '80s mullet haircut.

While dating apps like Tinder, Bumble and Hinge might remain the largest pools for wholesale, bulk swiping, there's a long tail of niche options for daters who already know exactly what they're looking for.

So, whether you can't live without a partner who loves death metal or desperately need to marry a fellow millionaire, there's a dating app or site out there catering to your own, very specific community of singles.

Here are ten of the most unusual dating services online right now.

To each their own.

Singles With Food Allergies

A dinner date with an attractive stranger can be stressful enough without having to consider anyone's life-threatening dietary restrictions. 

Enter 'Singles with Food Allergies' (SWFA), a $14.95-per-month subscription dating site for finding a soulmate who shares your same food allergy. 'Celiac & sexy?' the site's description asks. 'Dairy-free & dynamic?' 

'Nowhere else will you find so many divorced, never-married or widowed individuals whose lives are all affected by food allergies,' boasts the site's founder, Matty Thomas.

Thomas created SWFA to fill a need in his own allergy-challenged life following a divorce, but the creator's recommendations to new members revealed a few unrelated, no less laser-specific expectations.

'Write complete words,' Thomas advised, 'not IM slang.'

Ugly Schmucks

It's hard out there for singles everywhere, but as the brains behind Ugly Schmucks note 'especially if you're like a lot of us who are not that attractive.' 

Schmucks describes itself as an 'elite ugly dating site.' The pitch is an online community just for 'people who may feel unattractive or uncomfortable in their own skin' — and those who love them, because they 'value genuine personality over outer appearance.'

The site also says that it's 'exclusive' but, as the Loving.is dating database points out, Ugly Schmucks requires no email confirmation to register and doesn't verify photos. Naturally, this has left the community wide open to fake accounts given its mission statement, which is already ripe for pranks.

Or, as one user put it last summer, 'So weird that only good-looking people are on this dating website?'

Mullet Passions 

Business in the front, party in the back.  

Whether your long-maned, short-topped hairdo is a nostalgic throwback, a cheeky ironic come-on, or just the hairstyle that you have had without interruption since 1985, Michael Carter's Mullet Passions promises to connect you with singles who'll love your lustrous locks. 

For daters who can't wait to see their future soulmate's mullet in motion, the site offers a 24/7 'Mullet Lover' webcam chat, with the chaste warning that 'no sexual content or activity is allowed.' 

Carter's Mullet is just one of over 260 sites from his Passions Network of niche dating communities, which includes sites for lovers of Star Trek, scuba diving, the Green Party, Wiccans, and hundreds more. 

Carter founded Passions in 2004 after hosting a simple dating directory, cataloging the many side streets of online dating. 

'I wanted to have the destination sites people sought out and not just run a directory that sent people to other sites,' Michael said.

Stache Passions

Another follicle-based Carter specialty, Stache Passions promises to help people find partners with the facial hair of their dreams.

Stache Passions even has a variety of mustache subgroups, so that singles can browse 'to find members by their preferred type of 'stache, whether it be Dali, Walrus or Pencil.'

Like Mullet Passions, the site offers a 24/7 webcam chat for those who need to get live-streamed confirmation of what their prospective partner's upper lip is working with. Once again, of course, 'no sexual content,' no virtual mustache rides, allowed.

Date My Pet

Some pet owners will swear that their animal is a great judge of people's character, but few dating sites make that vetting process more explicit than datemypet.com.

No one wants to bring someone home, only to find out they are allergic to dogs, or to discover that their cat can't stop hissing at their new prospective partner.

With a broad mandate, datemypets.com hopes to be 'not just another dating site,' but a community for all pet owners who are 'looking for love or friendship.' 

It even will accept members who don't have pets, so long as they remember that, when push comes to shove, every pet owner on the site has one hard rule: 'If you want to date me, you have to date my pet.'

Clown Dating promises to match any interested 'Clown Lover,' 'Clown Wannabe' or professional working 'Clown' with the lovable, red-nosed goofball that's right for them, which can be a challenge for touring performers

Clown Dating 

Some people are afraid of clowns, but the premise behind Clown Dating is that some just might want to love one, or maybe be excited to face their fears head-on.

The dating site promises to match any interested 'Clown Lover,' 'Clown Wannabe' or professional working 'Clown' with the lovable, red-nosed goofball that's right for them, which can be a challenge for touring performers.

Truly, it's impossible to underplay the international community that Clown Dating has brought together. 

The profiles include residents from all over the world, from Madagascar to New York; from Nice along the French Riviera to Doha, Qatar.

'Being on the road all the time can make it hard to find someone close by,' the Clown Dating site notes, 'but with members from towns and cities all over the world hooking up has never been easier.'

MillionaireMatch

By internet standards, wanting to date exclusively within your own, elite, seven-figure-and-above tax bracket sounds positively vanilla, but MillionaireMatch's unique selling point is that it got there first.

Founded by UC Berkeley graduate Jason Du in 2001, MillionaireMatch was the first site to emerge with the unique premise of helping the wealthy find each other. So, naturally it rose up out of the Dot Com boom in Silicon Valley. 

Premium users are asked to submit a state-issued ID, tax returns and property info to prove their millionaire income claims

'I was curious why so many of my friends and coworkers, who were very successful people, were still single,' Du said in a 2015 interview.  

'When I asked them, they said: a) they were too busy, b) it was hard to find people matching their criteria and c) they didn't want to date gold diggers.' 

For verification, premium users are asked to submit not only a state-issued ID, but tax returns and property info to prove that their millionaire income claims are true. 

Passive wealth, notwithstanding, a dater must also have an annual income of at least $200,000 to qualify for a 'verified' badge.

Metal Dating promises to connect single hard rockers, with more than just dedicated profiles and messaging, but also the chance to start a blog or post shows to an events calendar to really broadcast the bands and music you love

Metal Dating 

It's a long way to the top if you want to rock'n'roll, as AC/DC once sang. And it can be a lonely way up without that special someone.  

Metal Dating promises to fix that, with more than just dedicated profiles and messaging, but also the chance to start a blog or post shows to an events calendar to really broadcast the bands and music you love.  

One woman gushed on the 'success stories' section that she had found her 'absolute soulmate' on the site. 

'Fast forward a bit,' she wrote, 'and we are talking marriage and buying a house together.' 

Nothing is more metal than homeownership.

Can Do Better

Let's say you're not single, but looking to get back out there.

The unsentimental, Darwinian premise of Can Do Better is that you just might want to let an anonymous jury of your peers decide whether you or your current partner is the one who belongs in a higher league. 

Structured closely to the web1.0 staple 'Hot or Not,' the site that inspired Mark Zuckerberg's site before Facebook, Can Do Better's voting system has been delivering harsh truths to its user base since 2008. 

'We understand singles are searching for quality, not quantity,' the CEO of Blast Applications Inc., Dino Luzzi, said when the site was relaunched in 2010. 

'CanDoBetter.com increases the odds of finding a suitable dating partner.'
