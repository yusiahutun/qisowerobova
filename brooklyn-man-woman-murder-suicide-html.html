Brooklyn man and woman, both 34, are found dead in 'murder-suicide' with handgun near his body

 Posted on February 19, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  444 words  |  Martina Birk

A man and a woman have been found dead in their NYC apartment with gunshot wounds to their heads in a possible murder-suicide.

The pair were discovered after police were called to the building at 2nd Street and 5th Avenue in Park Slope, Brooklyn, at 1am on Thursday.

The couple, both 34, were pronounced dead at the scene. Police said they were 'dating'.

Jason Jackson, who describes himself as a singer, songwriter and chef, was identified as the man found dead

Police said the man's wound appeared to be self-inflicted, and there was a black handgun lying next to his body.

Family discovered the shocking scene after they hired a locksmith to open the door when they didn't hear from one of them.

The dead man was identified by multiple sources as Jason Jackson, who describes himself as a singer, songwriter and chef.

Police said the man's wound appeared to be self-inflicted, and there was a black handgun lying next to his body.

His brother, Foster Jackson, told NY Daily News his loved one had 'a voice like an angel.'

'He was a good kid and sweet boy, and very liked in the neighborhood,' Foster said.

His childhood friend, Jerome, told DailyMail.com at the crime scene that he was shocked to hear how Jackson's life ended.

'This is not the Jason I knew. I don't know what happened,' he said.

'I've known him my whole life, ever since he was born. He was the most beautiful kid I knew, handsome, funny – his whole family is funny.

'I never thought it would be this.

'This is devastating for both families. I offer my condolences to the woman's family first and foremost. I hope everyone gets the healing that they need.

'It's really tough. I wish it never happened, he's a brother to all of us in the neighborhood… it's shaken up all of Park Slope.'

Jerome, 41, said Jackson lived in the area since before it was the sought-after neighborhood it was today.

'You know Park Slope as this upper-echelon neighborhood, but for us, our families own these brownstones and it wasn't always what it was today.'

Three homicide detectives arrived about 11.40am and climbed the stairs to walk-up apartment where the couple was found. 

They only stayed about half an hour before leaving and didn't answer questions from reporters.
