    How to Choose the Best Coconut Oil for Hair?

    Here are a few aspects to consider when buying coconut oil for hair - 

    Hair Type: Coconut oil can be added to every hair type's regimen be it thin, thick, or coarse hair. If you're looking for an oil to soothe damaged, dry, and brittle hair, coconut oil is a perfect choice. Caution! Be it any hair type, over-applying any hair oil can make your scalp greasy.

    Formula: Choosing cold-pressed coconut oil is better for your hair care routine since it is considered to have more nutrients than expeller-pressed. Cold-pressed products are a good choice because these oils do not use heat or chemicals for extraction. 

    Price: Yes, the price tag plays a major role. Though sold at very cheap prices in tropical regions due to its vast harvest, the coconut oils sold across the globe may differ in terms of pricing. At times, the cost may reflect its ingredients and quality, so you can choose the best coconut oil for hair without it being too expensive.

    Benefits: Nutrients and fatty acids found in coconut oil are great for hair health. Our contributor Kate Ross, says, “Sometimes, other helpful things are added to coconut oil to make your hair even happier. For example, some oils like argan oil have extra vitamins which enrich coconut oil, or tea tree oil, which is good for your scalp.” It is used to treat dandruff, revive dull, damaged hair, reduce frizz, and shield hair from heat. Also, for thicker and longer hair, it aids in clearing sebum buildup.

    Certifications: Opt for oils that have organic, non-GMO, or fair trade certifications. This way, you rest assured that you're choosing both ethical and sustainable options.

    Fractionated Oil: If you prefer a coconut oil that has a more lightweight formula, then we suggest that you opt for a fractionated oil. It will get easily absorbed into the hair without leaving behind a greasy feeling.

    RBD Label: An RBD (Refined, Bleached, and Deodorized) label means that the oil you’re choosing is a refined coconut oil. If you want a more natural option, you should go for virgin or unrefined oils.

    Cold/Expeller-pressed: Cold-pressed or expeller-pressed oils have the capacity to retain a higher percentage of natural nutrients when compared to oils that are extracted using heat.

    Color: Coconut oil in its natural form is usually white or off-white. This way you can know if the oil you're choosing is refined or unrefined.

    How to Use the Best Coconut Oil for Hair in Daily Routine?

    Here are a few tips to rightly apply coconut oil for hair on an everyday basis - 

  • Prewash Protector: Applying coconut oil on the hair roots before washing your hair may help to reduce the amount of water that your hair absorbs, thereby decreasing the likelihood that your hair will get damaged or dry.
  • Conditioner: You can use coconut oil as an alternative to standard conditioners. Or add a few drops of the oil to your usual conditioner to boost its smoothing effect
  • Hairstyling Essential: Just a dab of coconut oil can go a long way when it comes to taming frizzy hair. For easier absorption and styling, make sure to apply the oil to damp hair.

    • Who Should Use Coconut Oil?

    People with the below-mentioned hair types and issues can greatly benefit by using coconut oil. Let's take a look at how:

    Dry And Damaged Hair

    Coconut oil has the power to retain moisture, and this makes it great for hair that's dry and damaged. It improves the elasticity of the strands, thus reducing the amount of hair breakage.

    Oily Hair

    Color-treated Hair

    Hair Growth And Thickness

    FAQ's

    How long should you leave coconut oil in your hair for the best results?

    Coconut oil can be left in your mane for no more than thirty minutes. 15 minutes should be plenty if you have weak or severely porous hair.

    How many times can you put coconut oil in your hair?

    An ideal number would be two to three times a week.

    Is coconut oil good for damaged hair?

    Yes. If you're looking for a natural solution for damaged, dry, and brittle hair, coconut oil is a fantastic choice.

    Can coconut oil damage your hair?

    If you use coconut oil excessively, it can lead to hair damage as it weighs down the hair and can be hard to wash out. This may lead to greasiness and buildup.

    Is overnight oiling good for hair?

    Oiling your hair overnight may be beneficial as it lets the oil deeply penetrate and nourish the hair and scalp. However, it's important that you don't overdo this and it can lead to greasiness and be difficult to wash out.

    How often should I put coconut oil in my hair?

    The frequency of applying coconut oil entirely depends on the individual's hair texture, type, and needs. However, generally, it can be used once or twice a week.

    Does coconut oil make hair white?

    No, coconut oil is not linked to the hair turning white. However, when applied excessively, the residue can make it appear greasy and dull.

    Is it OK to not oil hair?

    Yes, it's perfectly fine if you do not wish to oil your hair.

    Is coconut oil suitable for all hair types?

    Though coconut oil can be used on all hair types, the results that it offers may vary from person to person. Individuals with normal to dry hair will be able to get the most out of using coconut oil.
